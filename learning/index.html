Featured Tool

Assess & Align

In development

A tool built by the Alliance to help Black voluntary sector leaders evaluate research requests for equity, collaboration, and cultural sensitivity.

Born from the manifesto’s demand for equitable research practices (Recommendation 12), Assess & Align provides a structured framework for community organisations to assess whether a proposed research partnership genuinely serves their community — or simply extracts from it.

The tool helps organisations ask the right questions before committing to research relationships:

  • Is the research question shaped by the community it affects?
  • Is funding and ownership shared equitably?
  • Are findings accessible to participants, not just academics?
  • Does the methodology respect cultural context?
  • Is there accountability for how findings are used?

Launching 2026. Built by Alliance members with lived experience of navigating inequitable research partnerships.

Looking Ahead

The Burke Platform

Looking ahead, the Alliance is developing The Burke Platform — an AI-powered knowledge platform that will transform how Black civil society organisations access and use research evidence.

Named after Dr Aggrey Burke, a pioneer in transcultural psychiatry in Britain, the platform builds on the Synergi Collaborative Centre’s legacy of evidence-based work on ethnic inequalities in mental health.

The vision: a single place where Black-led organisations can find, understand, and apply the evidence that matters to their communities — without needing to navigate paywalled journals or decode academic language.

Early development stage. More details to follow.
