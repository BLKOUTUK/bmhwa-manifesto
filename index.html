Black people are detained under the Mental Health Act at nearly 4 times the rate of white people.

This must end.

The Black Mental Health Manifesto demands structural change. 23 recommendations. 3 areas of action. A coalition of people and organisations saying: enough.

Never only about race.

Black mental health lives where being Black meets being queer, disabled, a woman, a man, a migrant, of faith — and asks to be met in full.

The Crisis in Numbers

The evidence is clear

23%

Black adults experiencing a common mental health problem weekly — compared to 17% of White British adults.

NHS Digital, 2016

6%

Black adults receiving treatment for mental health problems — compared to 13% of White British adults.

NHS Digital, 2016

3.6x

Higher detention rate under the Mental Health Act for Black people compared to white people.

NHS Digital, 2023

£118bn

Annual cost of mental ill-health to the UK economy. Addressing disparities means cost savings for everyone.

McDaid et al, 2022

The Manifesto

3 Areas of Action

23 recommendations structured around the systemic, institutional, and cultural changes needed to close the inequity gap.

Part 1

Addressing Structural Drivers

8 recommendations targeting government, commissioners, and Integrated Care Systems. Tackle racism as a determinant of health.

Improving Ways of Working

9 recommendations for the NHS, regulators, and practitioners. Embed antiracist frameworks and conditions for accountability within health systems and structures.

Read recommendations 9–17 → Part 3

Reimagining Mental Health Support

6 recommendations to replace deficit-based Eurocentric understandings of mental health with care rooted in transformative justice, self-determination, community, and lived experience.
