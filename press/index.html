Key Facts

Key facts for journalists

The following statistics are drawn from the manifesto and its cited sources. All are suitable for publication with the source citations provided.

  • Black people are detained under the Mental Health Act at 3.6 times the rate of white people.

    NHS Digital, Mental Health Act Statistics, 2022/23

  • Only 6% of Black adults receive treatment for mental health problems, compared to 13% of White British adults — despite higher prevalence.

    NHS Digital, Adult Psychiatric Morbidity Survey, 2016

  • Black men are 10 times more likely to experience psychosis than white men (3.2% vs 0.3%).

    NHS Digital, APMS, 2016

  • 9 in 10 BAME-led charities were at risk of closure within the first three months of the COVID-19 pandemic.

    Voice4Change England & ACEVO, 2020

  • Black people are almost 9 times more likely to be stopped and searched by police. Negative police interactions increase risks of PTSD and depression.

    Home Office, Stop and Search Statistics, 2023

  • Mental ill-health costs the UK economy £118 billion annually through lost productivity, benefit payments, and health and social care costs.

    McDaid et al, London School of Economics, 2022
