## 1. Covering note to Ariel

**To:** Ariel Breaux (dracbreaux@gmail.com)
**Subject:** Manifesto site — ready for the group's eyes, and a favour to ask

Hi Ariel,

The redesigned site is ready for the steering group to look at properly: https://blkoutuk.github.io/bmhwa-manifesto

Two things have landed since you last saw it. The intersectionality statement now anchors the home page — it earned its own space rather than sharing a band with the language list. And there's a new section: an evidence library at https://blkoutuk.github.io/bmhwa-manifesto/evidence/ — 49 entries so far, each one summarised in plain language with a "what this means for your organisation" line, searchable, every source checked. It sits alongside Assess & Align, the tool that helps organisations weigh up research partnership requests: https://blkoutuk.github.io/align

Three asks for the group, whenever the moment fits:

**Feedback.** Anything that reads wrong, lands wrong, or is missing — we'd rather hear it now, while the site is still a draft.

**The languages question, on its own terms.** We cut the twelve-language row — it was carrying more weight than a list can hold. The question that remains is whether the site should acknowledge the many mother tongues across our communities, and if so, how. That deserves the group's judgement, not a design default.

**Circulation.** Below is a short note steering members can forward to member organisations as-is. The library and the tool only prove themselves in use — a real funding bid, a real commissioning conversation, a real research request. If each steering member sent it to one or two organisations who'd genuinely use it, we'd learn more in a month than from any amount of polishing.

Anything that comes back — reactions, corrections, stories of use — send it my way and we'll fold it in.

Rob

## 2. Forwardable note (for steering members → member orgs)

**Subject:** Two tools for your next funding bid or research request

The Black Mental Health & Wellbeing Alliance has been building something we'd like you to try.

**An evidence library** — the research and data on Black mental health, gathered in one place and translated into plain language. Each entry tells you what the evidence says and what it means for your organisation — for a funding bid, a commissioning conversation, a board paper. No paywalls, no jargon.
https://blkoutuk.github.io/bmhwa-manifesto/evidence/

**Assess & Align** — a five-minute tool for when a university or research team asks to partner with you. Ten questions that surface who holds the power, the purse, and the pen — and a feedback letter you can send back to them. Nothing you enter leaves your browser.
https://blkoutuk.github.io/align

Both are working drafts. That's deliberate — we want them shaped by real use, not polished in private. So the ask is simple: **next time you're writing a bid, weighing a commissioning decision, or looking at a research request, use them — then tell us what happened.** What helped, what was missing, what you'd change.

Replies to this email, or direct to Rob Berkeley at BLKOUT, who is building this with us: rob@blkoutuk.com
