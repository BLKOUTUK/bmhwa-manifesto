Care Quality Commission

State of Care 2024/25 — Black men's mental health

The regulator's own report: Black people are 3 to 5 times more likely to be diagnosed and admitted to hospital with schizophrenia than all other ethnic groups, and face inequalities along the entire care pathway — access, diagnosis, assessment, treatment and recovery. Stigma and the lack of culturally appropriate models are named as key barriers.

What this means for youWhen you tell a commissioner that mental health care reaches Black men late and unequally, this is the system's own inspector documenting it — in print.

Care Quality Commission

PCREF — what inspectors now look for

CQC's guide for its own inspectors and Mental Health Act reviewers on checking how providers implement the Patient and Carer Race Equality Framework. The NHS Standard Contract 2025-26 says mental health providers must implement PCREF; failure to implement it may indicate institutional racism or closed-culture risks, and lack of progress may affect a provider's ratings.

What this means for youLeverage. PCREF is contractual and inspected — a trust that treats race equality co-production as optional is now answerable to its regulator, and your organisation is exactly who they need to co-produce with.

Friendship Bench / King's College London

The Friendship Bench — community-delivered therapy that works

In Zimbabwe, lay community health workers — mainly grandmothers — deliver problem-solving therapy on benches outside primary care clinics. In a randomised controlled trial, people who received the therapy were more than three times less likely to have depression symptoms after six months than those receiving standard care (14% against 50%).

What this means for youRandomised-controlled-trial evidence that trained community members, not just clinicians, can deliver mental health care that works — the citation for community-delivered support in any bid.

Centre for Mental Health

Growing the Grassroots — what happens when funding actually reaches community organisations

An evaluation of South East London Mind's grants (£5,000–£10,000) and sub-contracting to community organisations supporting marginalised groups in Greenwich and Lewisham: 66% of people using the funded services said they would not know where else to go, and a further 20% said nowhere else existed. Grants proved more flexible and supportive than traditional commissioning for small organisations.

What this means for youEvidence that small grants to community organisations fill gaps nothing else fills — hand it to any larger charity or ICB deciding how to reach marginalised and racialised communities.

NHS England Digital

Mental Health Act detentions 2024-25 — the differences between groups

The official statistics: detention rates for the 'Black or Black British' group (262.4 per 100,000) were nearly four times those of the White group (65.8) — and for people recorded as 'Any Other Black Background', 780.3 per 100,000, twelve times the White British rate. Detention rates in the most deprived areas ran over three and a half times those in the least deprived.

The Trevor Project

2024 UK survey on the mental health of LGBTQ+ young people

Over 9,600 LGBTQ+ young people aged 13–24 across the UK: 58% seriously considered suicide in the past year and 19% attempted it. Two-thirds reported discrimination due to sexual orientation (66%) or gender identity (68%). Young people in schools very accepting of LGBTQ+ people attempted suicide at less than half the rate of those in non-accepting schools — 13% against 35%.

What this means for youThe scale evidence for any work with Black queer young people — and proof that affirming, accepting spaces are protective, not just kind.

LGBT Foundation

LGBTQ+ patient experiences in primary care

Survey evidence on LGBTQ+ experiences at the GP: 56% said their GP met their needs as an LGBTQ+ person, down from 68% in 2018 — and only 50% of queer, trans and intersex people of colour said so, against 57% of white respondents.
